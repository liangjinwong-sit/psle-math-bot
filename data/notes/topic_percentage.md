# Topic: Percentage

## Key Ideas
- Percentage means "out of 100".
- The symbol for percentage is %.
- Percentages can be used to compare parts of a whole.
- To find a percentage of a quantity, convert the percentage into a fraction or decimal and then multiply.
- Increase means add more.
- Decrease means subtract some amount.
- Always check whether the question is asking for the percentage, the part, or the whole.

## Standard Methods

### Method 1: Find a percentage of a quantity
1. Write the percentage as a fraction over 100.
2. Multiply by the quantity.
3. Simplify if needed and write the correct unit.

Example: 25% of 80 = 25/100 × 80 = 20

### Method 2: Find the whole when a percentage is given
1. Identify the known part and its percentage.
2. Divide the known part by the percentage value written as a fraction or decimal.
3. Check that the answer makes sense.

Example: If 20% of a number is 10, then the whole is 10 ÷ 0.2 = 50

### Method 3: Find percentage increase
1. Find the amount of increase.
2. Divide the increase by the original amount.
3. Multiply by 100%.

Example: Increase from 40 to 50 is 10, so percentage increase = 10 ÷ 40 × 100% = 25%

### Method 4: Find percentage decrease
1. Find the amount of decrease.
2. Divide the decrease by the original amount.
3. Multiply by 100%.

Example: Decrease from 60 to 45 is 15, so percentage decrease = 15 ÷ 60 × 100% = 25%

### Method 5: Find what percentage one quantity is of another
1. Divide the part by the whole.
2. Multiply by 100%.
3. Write the answer with the % sign.

Example: 15 out of 60 = 15 ÷ 60 × 100% = 25%

## Question Patterns
- If the question asks for a percentage of a quantity, multiply the quantity by the percentage.
- If the question says "what is 30% of 90", find the part from the whole.
- If the question gives a part and its percentage, divide to find the whole.
- If the question uses words like increased by or decreased by, find the change first.
- If the question asks "what percent", divide the part by the whole and multiply by 100%.
- If the question compares old and new values, use the original amount for percentage increase or decrease.

## Alternative Phrasings
- out of 100 = percent = percentage
- increased by = went up by
- decreased by = went down by
- part of the whole = percentage of a quantity
- what percent = find the percentage
- original amount = starting amount
- final amount = new amount

## Common Pitfalls
- Dividing when the question asks for a percentage of a quantity.
- Using the new amount instead of the original amount for percentage increase or decrease.
- Forgetting that percentage means out of 100.
- Mixing up the part and the whole.
- Writing the answer without the % sign when the answer is a percentage.
- Adding or subtracting before finding the percentage when the method should be multiplication first.

## Worked Example 1
Question: Find 25% of 80.

Step 1: Write 25% as 25/100.
Step 2: Multiply 25/100 × 80.
Step 3: 25/100 × 80 = 20.

Answer: 20

Why this works: 25% means 25 out of every 100, so we multiply the quantity by 25/100.

## Worked Example 2
Question: 30% of a number is 18. Find the number.

Step 1: Write 30% as 0.3.
Step 2: Let the number be the whole.
Step 3: Divide 18 by 0.3.
Step 4: 18 ÷ 0.3 = 60.

Answer: 60

Why this works: If 30% of the whole is known, divide the part by 0.3 to find the whole.

## Worked Example 3
Question: The price of a shirt increased from $40 to $50. Find the percentage increase.

Step 1: Find the increase: 50 - 40 = 10.
Step 2: Divide by the original price: 10 ÷ 40 = 0.25.
Step 3: Convert to percentage: 0.25 × 100% = 25%.

Answer: 25%

Why this works: Percentage increase compares the increase with the original amount.

## Worked Example 4
Question: The number of books decreased from 80 to 60. Find the percentage decrease.

Step 1: Find the decrease: 80 - 60 = 20.
Step 2: Divide by the original number: 20 ÷ 80 = 0.25.
Step 3: Convert to percentage: 0.25 × 100% = 25%.

Answer: 25%

Why this works: Percentage decrease compares the decrease with the original amount.

## Worked Example 5
Question: 15 out of 60 students are in the choir. What percentage of the students are in the choir?

Step 1: Divide the part by the whole: 15 ÷ 60 = 0.25.
Step 2: Convert to percentage: 0.25 × 100% = 25%.

Answer: 25%

Why this works: To find what percent, compare the part with the whole and convert to a percentage.

## Quick Practice

- A class had 40 pupils and now has 30 pupils. Find the percentage decrease.
- 18 out of 90 marbles are red. What percentage are red?
