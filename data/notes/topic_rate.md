# Topic: Rate and Unitary Reasoning

## Key Ideas
- A rate compares two quantities with different units.
- Examples of rates include km/h, dollars per item, and litres per bottle.
- "Per unit" means for 1 unit.
- Unitary method means finding the value of 1 unit first.
- Many multi-step word problems can be solved by finding the value of 1 unit first.
- Always include the correct unit in the final answer.

## Standard Methods

### Method 1: Find a unit rate
1. Identify the total amount and total number of units.
2. Divide by the number of units.
3. Write the answer with the correct unit.

Example: 5 notebooks cost $15, so 1 notebook costs 15 ÷ 5 = $3

### Method 2: Find total amount from a unit rate
1. Find the amount for 1 unit.
2. Multiply by the required number of units.

Example: If 1 apple costs $2, then 6 apples cost 6 × 2 = $12

### Method 3: Find speed
1. Use speed = distance ÷ time.
2. Keep the units correct.

Example: 180 km in 3 hours means 180 ÷ 3 = 60 km/h

### Method 4: Solve multi-step unitary reasoning problems
1. Find the value of 1 unit first.
2. Scale up or down to the amount needed.
3. Check the final unit.

Example: 8 bottles hold 12 litres, so 1 bottle holds 12 ÷ 8 = 1.5 litres

### Method 5: Find quantity from total and rate
1. Identify the total amount and rate.
2. Divide the total by the rate.
3. Check the unit of the answer.

Example: If 20 dollars buys apples at $4 each, then 20 ÷ 4 = 5 apples

## Question Patterns
- If the question asks for the cost of 1 item, divide total cost by number of items.
- If the question asks "per hour", "per item", "per bottle", or "each", find the unit rate first.
- If the question gives distance and time, divide distance by time to find speed.
- If the question asks for total cost after giving the cost of 1 item, multiply.
- If the question gives a total amount shared equally, divide by the number of equal groups.
- If the question uses words like "average speed", "each", or "one", the unitary method is often useful.

## Alternative Phrasings
- cost of 1 = unit cost = per item = each item
- per hour = each hour
- speed = rate
- average speed = distance divided by time
- equally shared = divided equally
- amount for 1 = unit rate

## Common Pitfalls
- Multiplying when you should divide to find 1 unit.
- Forgetting to write the unit in the final answer.
- Mixing up distance, time, and speed.
- Using inconsistent units.
- Skipping the 1-unit step in a multi-step problem.
- Finding the cost for many items when the question asks for the cost of 1 item.

## Worked Example 1
Question: 5 notebooks cost $15. How much does 1 notebook cost?

Step 1: Divide the total cost by the number of notebooks.
Step 2: 15 ÷ 5 = 3.
Step 3: Write the unit.

Answer: 1 notebook costs $3.

Why this works: The unitary method finds the value of 1 item first.

## Worked Example 2
Question: A car travels 180 km in 3 hours. What is its average speed?

Step 1: Use speed = distance ÷ time.
Step 2: 180 ÷ 3 = 60.
Step 3: Write the unit as km/h.

Answer: 60 km/h

Why this works: Speed tells us how much distance is covered in 1 hour.

## Worked Example 3
Question: 8 bottles hold 12 litres of water in total. How much water does 1 bottle hold?

Step 1: Divide the total volume by the number of bottles.
Step 2: 12 ÷ 8 = 1.5.
Step 3: Write the unit.

Answer: 1.5 litres

Why this works: To find the amount for 1 bottle, divide the total equally among 8 bottles.

## Worked Example 4
Question: 6 pens cost $18. How much does 1 pen cost?

Step 1: Divide the total cost by the number of pens.
Step 2: 18 ÷ 6 = 3.
Step 3: Write the unit.

Answer: 1 pen costs $3.

Why this works: The cost of 1 pen is found by sharing the total cost equally among 6 pens.

## Worked Example 5
Question: A bus travels 240 km in 4 hours. Find its average speed.

Step 1: Use speed = distance ÷ time.
Step 2: 240 ÷ 4 = 60.
Step 3: Write the unit as km/h.

Answer: 60 km/h

Why this works: Average speed is the distance travelled in 1 hour.

## Quick Practice
- 9 boxes contain 45 oranges. How many oranges are in 1 box?
- If 1 ticket costs $7, how much do 5 tickets cost?
- 12 litres of juice are poured equally into 6 bottles. How much juice is in each bottle?
- A van travels 150 km in 3 hours. What is its average speed?
- 4 loaves of bread cost $12. How much does 1 loaf cost?
- If 1 pencil costs $2, how much do 8 pencils cost?
