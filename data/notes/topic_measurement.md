# Topic: Measurement

## Key Ideas
- Perimeter is the total distance around a shape.
- Area is the amount of surface inside a shape.
- Volume is the amount of space inside a solid.
- Different measurements use different units.
- Perimeter is usually measured in units such as cm or m.
- Area is measured in square units such as cm² or m².
- Volume is measured in cubic units such as cm³ or m³.
- Always check whether the question is asking for perimeter, area, or volume.

## Standard Methods

### Method 1: Find the perimeter of a rectangle
1. Add the length and width.
2. Multiply the sum by 2.
3. Write the correct unit.

Example: Perimeter = 2 × (8 + 5) = 26 cm

### Method 2: Find the area of a rectangle
1. Multiply the length by the width.
2. Write the answer in square units.

Example: Area = 8 × 5 = 40 cm²

### Method 3: Find the perimeter of a square
1. Multiply one side by 4.
2. Write the correct unit.

Example: Perimeter = 4 × 9 = 36 cm

### Method 4: Find the volume of a cuboid
1. Multiply the length, width, and height.
2. Write the answer in cubic units.

Example: Volume = 4 × 3 × 2 = 24 cm³

### Method 5: Solve composite figure area questions
1. Split the figure into simpler rectangles or squares.
2. Find the area of each part.
3. Add or subtract the parts as needed.

Example: Add the areas of two rectangles to get the total area of the composite shape

## Question Patterns
- If the question asks for the distance around a shape, find the perimeter.
- If the question asks for the amount of space inside a flat shape, find the area.
- If the question asks for the space inside a solid, find the volume.
- If the question gives length and width of a rectangle, area usually means multiply.
- If the question gives side length of a square and asks for perimeter, multiply by 4.
- If the question shows a complicated shape, split it into simpler parts first.

## Alternative Phrasings
- distance around = perimeter
- space inside a flat shape = area
- space inside a solid = volume
- rectangular figure = rectangle
- square units = units for area
- cubic units = units for volume
- split the figure = break the figure into parts

## Common Pitfalls
- Mixing up perimeter and area.
- Forgetting to use square units for area.
- Forgetting to use cubic units for volume.
- Adding numbers when the formula needs multiplication.
- Using the wrong unit in the final answer.
- Forgetting that perimeter is around the outside, not inside the shape.

## Worked Example 1
Question: A rectangle has length 8 cm and width 5 cm. Find its area.

Step 1: Use the formula area = length × width.
Step 2: Multiply 8 × 5.
Step 3: 8 × 5 = 40.

Answer: 40 cm²

Why this works: Area measures the number of square units needed to cover the shape.

## Worked Example 2
Question: A rectangle has length 9 cm and width 4 cm. Find its perimeter.

Step 1: Add the length and width: 9 + 4 = 13.
Step 2: Multiply by 2.
Step 3: 2 × 13 = 26.

Answer: 26 cm

Why this works: A rectangle has two lengths and two widths.

## Worked Example 3
Question: A cuboid has length 4 cm, width 3 cm, and height 2 cm. Find its volume.

Step 1: Use the formula volume = length × width × height.
Step 2: Multiply 4 × 3 = 12.
Step 3: Multiply 12 × 2 = 24.

Answer: 24 cm³

Why this works: Volume measures how many cubic units fill the solid.

## Worked Example 4
Question: A square has side length 9 cm. Find its perimeter.

Step 1: A square has 4 equal sides.
Step 2: Multiply 9 by 4.
Step 3: 9 × 4 = 36.

Answer: 36 cm

Why this works: Perimeter is the total length around the square.

## Worked Example 5
Question: A rectangle has area 24 cm² and width 4 cm. Find its length.

Step 1: Use area = length × width.
Step 2: Divide 24 by 4.
Step 3: 24 ÷ 4 = 6.

Answer: 6 cm

Why this works: If one side and the area are known, divide the area by the known side to find the missing side.
